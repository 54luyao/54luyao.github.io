Project Name
Overlap 2.0: A Multilayer Shell for Architectural Applications

2016
Tongji University

Team
Yao Lu, Yang Yang

Description: Built upon Overlap 1.0, this design research project explores the coordination of multiple shell layers and investigates how the spaces generated between them can be used for architectural applications. Using a community market as the program, the project develops a multilayer shell system that integrates structural form, spatial organization, circulation, and environmental performance. The overlapping shells create a range of shaded, semi-open, and enclosed spaces that support different scales of public activity, from market stalls and gathering areas to transitional spaces. Through this proposal, the shell is not treated only as a structural surface, but also as an architectural system capable of shaping space, movement, and social interaction.

Keywords
Graphic Statics, Structure, Architecture
