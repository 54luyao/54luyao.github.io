Description

In this comprehensive design studio, students will design a Community Dance Center — a place where movement, culture, structure, and light intersect. Students are encouraged to approach the project holistically, integrating architectural systems with a strong conceptual and structural agenda. Inspired by the spatial dynamism of dance, the studio explores how structural steel can serve not just as support, but as an expressive and transformative architectural medium.

Students will work in teams of 2-3. Each team must use steel as the primary structural material and include at least one long-span space that demonstrates innovative structural thinking. Students will develop an understanding of tectonic expression, material articulation, and spatial experience, while also coordinating building systems such as daylighting, MEP, circulation, and envelope design. Site responsiveness is critical, enabling students to focus on the interplay between structure and form in an urban context. The studio topic complies with the 2026 ACSA/AISC Steel Design Student Competition. All student works will be invited to register and submit for the competition in spring 2026.
