Description

This comprehensive design studio will work to understand how site design, envelope, structural, lighting and MEP systems integrate into a building. Central to the integration of these systems is the idea that you must first and foremost approach a building's design from a holistic position, one where many static and dynamic variables are viewed as interrelated elements and the project directly serves its occupants.

A modern interpretation of church is the integration of space, light, and structure. The style of iconic churches - gothic cathedrals for example - does not come from a preplanned notion of what a church should “look like”. It is a pure expression of structure reaching towards the heavens and sunlight as far as physically possible without collapse. The pointed arch is not a design motif, but a shape that directs structural loads around window openings smoothly while reducing outward thrust. Structure, space and light are seamlessly intertwined in the work. Antonio Gaudi's modern interpretations of gothic epitomize this overlap.
